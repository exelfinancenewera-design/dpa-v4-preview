Can I get down payment assistance with a 620 credit score?

620 is exactly the floor most state down payment assistance programs use, so a 620 score generally puts these programs within reach (FHA lending itself starts at 580). At $85,000 income with $350/month of debts, the affordability math supports a home around $299,188 — and clearing the 620 bar is what unlocks the assistance that can cover the $8,976 down payment on it.

Related questions

What credit score do you need for down payment assistance?

Most state programs set a 620 minimum; some FHA-based paths go to 580. Requirements are set by each agency and are often paired with income limits based on area median income.

Can I get assistance with a score below 620?

Sometimes — a few programs and FHA lenders work with 580+. But 620 opens far more options and better pricing, so a 20–40 point improvement is usually the highest-leverage move.

Does using assistance hurt my credit?

No. Applying involves a normal mortgage credit check. The assistance itself is a second loan or grant and does not damage your score.
