Michigan DPA — common questions

What down payment assistance is available in Michigan?

Michigan's main statewide option is the MSHDA MI 10K Down Payment Assistance Loan — forgivable second loan administered by the state housing finance agency. Many cities and counties in Michigan add their own programs on top, so it is worth checking all three levels.

How much house can I afford in Michigan with assistance?

It depends on your income, debts and credit. The calculator applies the FHA 31%/43% debt-to-income rule to estimate your price, then shows how much of your down-payment gap Michigan assistance could cover. Most buyers pair it with a 3% conventional or 3.5% FHA down payment.

What credit score do I need for Michigan down payment assistance?

Many state programs set a 620 minimum credit score alongside income limits based on area median income. Exact requirements are set by the agency — verify current terms on the official MSHDA MI 10K Down Payment Assistance Loan page before applying.
